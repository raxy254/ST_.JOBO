Our History

A legacy of excellence spanning over seven decades

St. Joseph's Boys High School Kitale has a rich and storied history dating back to 1952. From humble beginnings under Catholic sponsorship to becoming one of Kenya's premier national schools, our institution has remained committed to academic excellence, discipline, and leadership development.

1952

School Founded

The school was established under Catholic sponsorship, laying the foundation for what would become one of Kenya's most respected educational institutions. The founding vision emphasized holistic education grounded in strong moral values.

1960s

Structured Secondary School

The school transitioned into a structured secondary education system, expanding its curriculum and facilities to meet the growing demand for quality education in the region.

1988

Education System Change

The A-Level system was phased out nationally, and the school adapted to the new 8-4-4 education system, continuing to maintain high academic standards through the transition.

1993

Boys & Girls Split

The original mixed institution was split into separate boys and girls schools, allowing for more focused educational approaches and specialized programs for each gender.

2012

National School Status

St. Joseph's Boys High School Kitale was upgraded to National School status, recognizing its consistent academic performance, excellent facilities, and commitment to producing well-rounded graduates.

2020–2026

Consistent Excellence

The school has maintained outstanding KCSE performance year after year, consistently ranking among the top schools in Trans Nzoia County and Kenya at large. Our students continue to excel in academics, sports, and co-curricular activities.

Our Heritage

Catholic Foundation

Our Catholic heritage continues to influence our values and approach to education, emphasizing faith, service, and moral integrity.

Thousands of successful alumni serve as ambassadors of our school's values in various fields across Kenya and beyond.
